The Virus Will Win

The Virus Will Win It is clear to me that we are unable to stop the roaring Covid. This is not an AIDS where we can make choices to control viral exposures. It is far more contagious. Our feeble attempts to flatten the curve failed despite shutting down the US economy. Under that plan with no vaccine, all people who were going to catch Covid would catch it over a long slow period that would not overload the hospitals. That did not work. People became bored and complacent. They refused to wear those god awful masks and heaven forbid stay six feet away from each other. How is that too much to ask? Now vulnerable older people such as my wife and I, who would have caught Covid slowly, or maybe lasted until a vaccine came out, will catch it incredibly fast and overwhelm the medical system. Will this save the economy? No one knows. The same number of deaths may occur, just all at once like occurred in New York City. My only hope is to be a safe hermit until a vaccine comes out in a year or two for everybody. I certainly won’t qualify for an early shot. Those who don’t care about being sick will catch Covid and mostly live while spreading it to those of us who are weak. We are seeing Darwinism at its best. Or worst, as Mother Nature carries on with her periodic population control..

Escape

I just spent 20 days in a nursing home; watching over my mother as she convalesced and went through rehab. I was with her when she fell, and I figured that, since I ‘broke her’…well, you know. It’s hard not to observe the comings and goings of a place like that. This story describes one of the ‘goings’ I observed. Yes, I am guilty of a little dramatic license. I thought this story might be a little amusing, but it’s actually quite heartbreaking. He waited patiently among the crowd of people; who were loitering around the entrance. Today was the day of some kind of party, or gathering in the building; so he blended in somewhat. Was it his plan all along? Or, maybe these ‘ripe’ circumstances just demanded that he take advantage of the situation. It was the perfect storm of cover and confusion. Maybe the ‘powers that be’ felt he deserved a shot at slipping out. A shot at freedom; perhaps another opportunity to do all the things he had left undone in his otherwise full life. One moment he was blending in with the crowd; the next, he was out the door. One could only guess what was going through his mind as he realized that he had made it through. Past the barrier that had confounded so many; his mind raced now that he had broke that, previously unbreakable, plane. But, no sooner than he tasted this first fleeting moment of freedom, he found himself in the grasp of one of his captors. “STOP!” he probably heard, as a rough-speaking, heavy-handed jailer grabbed his arm and pulled. ‘Was there no code of conduct for these ‘monsters’, who were holding him against his will’, he thought; as another guard joined the first. All was lost. All was, indeed, lost as the ruthless animals (his eyes and mind were seeing), pulled him back into the facility. The actual attendants and voices, which were seen and heard by the lucid people witnessing the scene, were actually gentle and compassionate. “Come on honey… let’s go inside”, one said as he accompanied them back into his ‘prison’. “We’d miss you if you left us Mr. ‘Blank’," the other one added as they gently escorted him. As the three walked down the hallway, his mind slowed again and began to collect itself. You could almost hear the whirling and clinking inside his head as he began to formulate the intricate details of the next ‘great nursing home escape’. If you have a friend or loved one in a nursing home, VISIT THEM OFTEN!

Realization

Amanda and John, a dashing couple in mid thirties did not realize when their relationship turned toxic and the day came when John had to approach the judiciary. John cannot forget how Amanda would always find him at fault and take no responsibility for her erratic behavior. One day John booked tickets for a cantata at an Opera House and decided to surprise Amanda. Amanda looked beautiful that evening. When they reached the Opera House and approached their seats, Amanda got furious at John for not booking the front row seats. She darted out of the Opera House towards the street in hysteria. John could not understand why was she so fussy about it instead of being happy for the surprise. She started shouting at him on the streets. Seemed like the anger was gestating for a long time. John tried to calm her down but she was being so impersonal that he felt emasculated. John looked totally abashed. He realized that his every effort to please Amanda was nothing but wasting time and effort securing a bauble.

The tunnel of the unloved(Poem)

Pull me inside out and expose me to the world, let them gawk and stare at my vulnerability which I can no longer be bothered to hide and let them laugh at my inner pain, I no longer care. The tunnel of darkness has closed in on me again at one end, the flashing orange lights of warning on the barricade to tell me that mental maintenance is due and I'm to stay away for now, and the light at the other keeps moving, it keeps getting further and further away and I don't want to run after it right now, I'm too tired and I'm too heavy to move. I'll sit here quietly in my solitude and despair, I'll comfort myself with my loneliness because at least I know it's better to be alone than inflict myself upon those who do not understand what it feels like to be pressed so heavily in sadness, in this tunnel of never ending darkness. The walls have closed around me but they are keeping me safe. They are damp and insidious but they protect me from myself and whilst I am alone, whilst there is no one to hold my hand and wait with me, I can reboot my brain, let my imagination shut down and restart again knowing that the nightmares I have been enduring will have passed and the storm clouds will clear once again. How long for I don't know. A few days, a few weeks, if Im lucky, a month or maybe 2, it doesn't matter though, just as long as I can finally stand on my own and walk towards the light and stand outside the tunnel in the real world knowing that I survived another dark day. You can tell me to grow up, to stop complaining, that it's all in my head, that I have nothing to worry about. You can tell me I'm lucky, that I'm better off than most, that I have everything to live for and I'll tell you that I don't want to live, I want to hide and stay hidden, that I want to be forgotten and remembered for everything I didn't do. I am loved and neglected. Stable and falling apart. Happy and desperately lost. Cold and too hot to care. I am everything and nothing. I am high and falling down. I change like the weather, I am a storm that never ends. Some of my friends understand me, they listen to me go on and on about this and that and they never judge a word I say. Why should they? It's not their place to judge, just to be there when I need someone to hold me up when I feel like crumbling forever to the ground. Some of my friends tell me to cheer up, that life is not that bad and they berate me for giving in so easily, and I just smile and walk away, shaking my head in frustration as I wish it was as simple as that. Most people know why I suffer with crippling depression, but not all of them understand it. Some glare at me with shock and tell me to get help! They think I am mad, they wonder..."Did she actually say that or write that?" They are the ones who make me laugh, the ones I let think that I might actually be crazy. Some praise me for being as strong as I am, and some do not understand how I manage to keep going, how I haven't failed and given in to my weakness. I have done, I still do, I just don't lean on everyone as not everyone is strong enough to take it, so I hide in my tunnel of darkness, I sit quietly on my own, listening to the deafening storm inside my head and wait patiently for it to pass. Would you know how I suffer sometimes? How anyone like me suffers? Do you know how much physical pain this depression causes and how much anger I have to suppress just so I don't flip out in public places and cause a scene an embarrass other people? I have learned to stay hidden, learned to be quiet and hide myself away. I can survive on my own now when I thought I needed someone to help me but I really don't. I am happier knowing that I can retreat to the tunnel of the unloved any time I choose and no one can ask me why or where I'm going. I can just go and sit there for a while, reboot and reinstall my life back to it's factory settings, ready to face another day or 2, or 3, ready to present myself once more as a normal, balanced, untainted human being. This is written on a dark day. The sun is shining and I am with people I love and who love me, yet the day is as dark as though the world has come to an end. I slept for hours. I stared at the wall for ages. I forgot my name and who I was as I began to shut down. I have been in the tunnel this time for 3 days and whilst I'll be out soon, whilst the light at one end is coming closer, I'm not sure if and when I'll be ready to leave.
